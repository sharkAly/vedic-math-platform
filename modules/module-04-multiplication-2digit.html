4.1 Crosswise Method Basics

The method we use is called "Vertically and Crosswise". It has 3 simple steps for 2-digit numbers. Let's see how it works with 23 × 47.

The 3 Steps:
  1. Right Vertical: Multiply the units digits (3 × 7).
  2. Crosswise: Multiply across (2 × 7) + (3 × 4).
  3. Left Vertical: Multiply the tens digits (2 × 4).
Why This Works: It's basically algebra! (10a + b)(10c + d) = 100ac + 10(ad + bc) + bd.
  • bd is Step 1 (Vertical Right)
  • ad + bc is Step 2 (Crosswise)
  • ac is Step 3 (Vertical Left)

Worked Examples:

  1. 34 × 68:
    ① 4×8 = 32 (write 2, carry 3)
    ② (3×8)+(4×6) = 24+24=48. 48+3(carry)=51 (write 1, carry 5)
    ③ 3×6 = 18. 18+5(carry)=23
    Answer: 2,312
  2. 52 × 73:
    ① 2×3 = 6
    ② (5×3)+(2×7) = 15+14=29 (write 9, carry 2)
    ③ 5×7 = 35. 35+2=37
    Answer: 3,796

4.2 With Larger Numbers & Carries

When numbers get bigger (like 6, 7, 8, 9), you'll have more carries. The method is exactly the same, you just need to add the carry to the next step.

Important: Always finish the multiplication first, THEN add the carry. Don't add the carry before multiplying!

Example: 67 × 89

  1. Step 1: 7×9 = 63. Write 3, carry 6.
  2. Step 2: (6×9) + (7×8) = 54 + 56 = 110. Add carry (110+6=116). Write 6, carry 11.
  3. Step 3: 6×8 = 48. Add carry (48+11=59). Write 59.

Answer: 5,963

4.4 Near-50 Shortcuts

For numbers close to 50 (like 48 or 52), there's an even faster way using base 50.

The Technique:
For 48 × 52:
1. 48 is -2 from 50. 52 is +2 from 50.
2. Difference of squares: (50-2)(50+2) = 50² - 2² = 2500 - 4 = 2496.
It's instant!
