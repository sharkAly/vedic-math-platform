2.1 Complement Method (Base Subtraction)

Subtracting numbers from 100, 1000, or 10,000 is usually painful with borrowing. The Complement Method makes this instant.

The Concept: Instead of borrowing, find what number completes the value to the next base (10, 100, 1000).
Why This Works: Powers of 10 are just "1" followed by zeros. Instead of borrowing across all those zeros, we use the formula: Subtract all digits from 9, and the last digit from 10.

Worked Examples:

  1. 1000 - 237:
    • Digit 2: 9 - 2 = 7
    • Digit 3: 9 - 3 = 6
    • Digit 7: 10 - 7 = 3
    • Result: 763
  2. 100 - 47: 9-4=5; 10-7=3. Result: 53.
  3. 10000 - 8152: 9-8=1; 9-1=8; 9-5=4; 10-2=8. Result: 1848.
  4. 1000 - 58: Treat as 058. 9-0=9; 9-5=4; 10-8=2. Result: 942.
  5. 500 - 168: 5-1=4 (minus 1 extra) = 3; then apply complement to 68 (32). Result: 332.
Try This!
Calculate 1000 - 642.
9-6=? | 9-4=? | 10-2=?
(Answer: 358)
Common Mistake: Forgetting that "Last" means the last non-zero digit. If a number ends in zero (e.g., 230), apply the rule to 23, then append the zero.

Real-World Application: You pay with a $10.00 bill for an item costing $3.45. Change? 9-3=6; 9-4=5; 10-5=5. Change is $6.55.

2.2 "All from 9, Last from 10" Extended

This technique isn't just for 100 or 1000. It works for subtracting from any multiple of 10, or when numbers have many zeros.

Worked Examples:

  1. 3000 - 426:
    • Decrement first digit: 3 becomes 2.
    • Apply formula to 426: 9-4=5, 9-2=7, 10-6=4.
    • Result: 2574
  2. 500 - 27: 5->4. 027 -> 9-0=9, 9-2=7, 10-7=3. Result: 473.
  3. 70000 - 36: 7->6. 00036 -> 9-0=9, 9-0=9, 9-0=9, 9-3=6, 10-6=4. Result: 69964.
  4. 800 - 455: 8-4-1=3 (subtract first digit plus 1). Apply complement to 55 (45). Result: 345.
  5. 1000 - 340: Ignore last 0. 100-34 -> 9-3=6, 10-4=6. Append 0. Result: 660.

2.3 Left-to-Right Subtraction

Just like addition, subtraction is faster when done Left-to-Right. The key is to peek ahead to see if the next column will need a "borrow" (we call it a "reduction").

Worked Examples:

  1. 84 - 36:
    • 8 - 3 = 5. But look ahead: 4 < 6.
    • Reduce 5 to 4.
    • Take complement of 6 (which is 4) and add to 4. 4+4=8.
    • Result: 48.
  2. 53 - 28: 5-2=3 (reduce to 2). 10-8=2; 2+3=5. Result: 25.
  3. 71 - 49: 7-4=3 (reduce to 2). 9's friend is 1; 1+1=2. Result: 22.
  4. 92 - 45: 9-4=5 (reduce to 4). 5's friend is 5; 5+2=7. Result: 47.
  5. 65 - 23: 6-2=4 (no reduction needed as 5 > 3). 5-3=2. Result: 42.

2.4 No-Borrowing Techniques

For larger numbers, you can just write down the difference digit by digit, marking "bar numbers" (negative digits) for any column where the bottom is bigger. Then convert at the end.

Worked Examples:

  1. 423 - 157:
    • 4-1=3
    • 2-5 = -3 (write as bar-3)
    • 3-7 = -4 (write as bar-4)
    • Convert: 300 - 30 - 4 = 266.
  2. 812 - 345: 5, -3, -3. 500-33 = 467.
  3. 671 - 298: 4, -2, -7. 400-27 = 373.
  4. 925 - 476: 5, -5, -1. 500-51 = 449.
  5. 543 - 289: 3, -4, -6. 300-46 = 254.
