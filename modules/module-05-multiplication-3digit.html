Module 5: 3-Digit & Mixed Multiplication

Welcome to Module 5!

Now that you've mastered 2-digit multiplication, it's time to scale up. The "Vertically and Crosswise" method works for ANY number of digits. In this module, we'll conquer 3-digit and mixed-digit multiplication.

Learning Objectives:

  • Master 3-digit × 2-digit multiplication (5 steps)
  • Handle mixed digit operations (2×3, 4×2)
  • Tackle advanced 3-digit × 3-digit calculations
  • Apply these skills to real-world large number problems

5.1 3-Digit × 2-Digit Multiplication

When multiplying a 3-digit number (like 234) by a 2-digit number (like 67), we simply treat the 2-digit number as having a leading zero (067) OR use a specific 5-step pattern. Let's look at the 5-step pattern for 234 × 67.

The 5 Steps (Moving Right to Left):
  1. Right Vertical: 4 × 7
  2. Crosswise Right: (3 × 7) + (4 × 6)
  3. Star Step (Middle): (2 × 7) + (3 × 6)
  4. Crosswise Left: (2 × 6)
  5. Left Vertical: (Leading zero logic applies here, or just stop if no digits left) -> Wait! For 3x2, it's actually safer to treat 67 as 067 and do full 3x3 steps. Let's visualize it as 3-digit x 2-digit specific pattern.

Actually, let's treat it as 234 × 067. This makes it a standard 3x3 pattern which is easier to memorize.

  1. Step 1 (Right): 4×7
  2. Step 2 (Cross Right): (3×7) + (4×6)
  3. Step 3 (Star): (2×7) + (3×6) + (4×0)
  4. Step 4 (Cross Left): (2×6) + (3×0)
  5. Step 5 (Left): (2×0) -> usually 0, unless carry.
Common Mistake: Forgetting the cross-multiply with the leading zero! Always treat 67 as 067. This keeps the pattern consistent.

Worked Examples:

  1. 456 × 83: (Treat as 456 × 083)
    ① 6×3 = 18 (8, c1)
    ② (5×3)+(6×8) = 15+48=63. 63+1=64 (4, c6)
    ③ (4×3)+(5×8)+(6×0) = 12+40+0=52. 52+6=58 (8, c5)
    ④ (4×8)+(5×0) = 32+0=32. 32+5=37 (7, c3)
    ⑤ (4×0)=0. 0+3=3.
    Answer: 37,848

5.2 2-Digit × 3-Digit (Mixed Order)

Does it matter if the small number comes first? Not at all! 67 × 234 is the same as 234 × 67. Just visualize the smaller number with a leading zero and put it "under" or "over" the larger one mentally. The pattern is the same.

5.3 3-Digit × 3-Digit Advanced

This is the heavyweight division! Multiplying two 3-digit numbers is a 5-step process. It requires focus, but it's incredibly satisfying to do in one line.

The 5 Steps for ABC × DEF:
  1. C×F
  2. (B×F) + (C×E)
  3. (A×F) + (B×E) + (C×D) [The Star Step!]
  4. (A×E) + (B×D)
  5. A×D

5.4 4-Digit × 2-Digit

Extending the pattern is logical. For 4-digit x 2-digit, simply add a leading zero to the 2-digit number to make it 3-digits? No, that's not enough digits. Treat it as 4-digit x 4-digit where two digits are zero! Or simply sliding the 2-digit number across.

Let's use the sliding method (or treating as 4x4 with 00XX). 1234 × 56 becomes 1234 × 0056.
