7.1 Numbers Ending in 5

This is perhaps the most famous and easiest Vedic trick. If a number ends in 5, you can square it faster than you can write it down.

The Method (Ekadhikena Purvena):
  1. Take the digit(s) before the 5. Call this 'n'.
  2. Multiply 'n' by the next number up (n + 1).
  3. Write that result, followed by 25.

Example: 35²

  • 'n' is 3. Next number is 4.
  • 3 × 4 = 12.
  • Append 25 → 1225.
Why This Works:

(10n + 5)² = 100n² + 100n + 25 = 100n(n+1) + 25.
This algebraic proof shows why we multiply n(n+1) for the hundreds part and append 25.

Worked Examples:

  1. 25²: 2 × 3 = 6 | Append 25 → 625
  2. 45²: 4 × 5 = 20 | Append 25 → 2025
  3. 85²: 8 × 9 = 72 | Append 25 → 7225
  4. 95²: 9 × 10 = 90 | Append 25 → 9025
  5. 115²: 11 × 12 = 132 | Append 25 → 13225

7.2 Squaring Near 50

For numbers close to 50 (like 48 or 53), we use a special base method. The "Working Base" is 50, but the "Reference Base" is 100/2.

The Method (Yavadunam):
  1. Compare the number to 25. (Yes, 25! It's the "Golden Number" for Base 50).
  2. Find the deviation from 50 (e.g., 53 is +3, 48 is -2).
  3. Left Part: 25 + Deviation.
  4. Right Part: Square of the Deviation (must be 2 digits).

Example: 53²

  • Deviation is +3.
  • Left: 25 + 3 = 28.
  • Right: 3² = 09.
  • Answer: 2809.

7.3 Squaring Near 100

Squaring numbers near 100 is identical to the Near Base multiplication method in Module 6, but simpler because both numbers are the same!

The Method:
  1. Find the deviation from 100.
  2. Left Part: Number + Deviation.
  3. Right Part: Deviation squared (2 digits).

Example: 97² (Deviation -3)

  • Left: 97 - 3 = 94.
  • Right: 3² = 09.
  • Answer: 9409.

Example: 104² (Deviation +4)

  • Left: 104 + 4 = 108.
  • Right: 4² = 16.
  • Answer: 10816.

7.4 General Squaring Method

For any other number (like 23 or 67), we use the general 2-digit multiplication method (Vertically and Crosswise) or the algebraic formula (a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b².

The Method (Duplex):

For a number 'ab' (like 23):

  1. Left: a²
  2. Middle: 2 × a × b
  3. Right: b²

Example: 23²

  • a=2, b=3
  • Left: 2² = 4
  • Middle: 2 × 2 × 3 = 12
  • Right: 3² = 9
  • Combine (with carry): 4 | 12 | 9 → 4+1 | 2 | 9 → 529.
