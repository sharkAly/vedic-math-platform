8.1 Nikhilam Division Method

This method is perfect for dividing by numbers close to a base (like dividing by 9, 8, 98, 997). It converts division into multiplication and addition!

The Method:
  1. Find the complement of the divisor from the nearest base (e.g., for 9, complement is 1).
  2. Split the dividend based on the base zeros (e.g., for base 10, split last digit).
  3. Bring down the first digit.
  4. Multiply by the complement and add to the next digit. Repeat.

Example: 123 ÷ 9 (Base 10, Complement 1)

  • Split: 12 | 3
  • Bring down 1. (Quotient so far: 1)
  • 1 × 1 = 1. Add to next digit: 2+1 = 3. (Quotient: 13)
  • 3 × 1 = 3. Add to remainder part: 3+3 = 6.
  • Answer: 13 R 6.

8.2 Special Divisors (9, 11, 99, 111)

These numbers have unique properties that allow for almost instant division. We've seen 9, but 11 and 99 are just as easy.

Division by 11 Shortcut:

Instead of adding like with 9, we subtract!

Example: 135 ÷ 11

  • Split: 13 | 5
  • Bring down 1.
  • 13 - 1 = 12. (Quotient: 12)
  • Remainder: 5 - 2 = 3.
  • Answer: 12 R 3.

8.3 Division by 8, 7, 6, 5

For single-digit division, sometimes the fastest way is using multiplication complements or simple doubling tricks.

Division by 5 Trick:

Instead of dividing by 5, multiply by 2 and move the decimal left!

Example: 240 ÷ 5

  • 240 × 2 = 480.
  • Move decimal: 48.0 → Answer: 48.
Division by 8 Trick:

Halve it three times!

Example: 120 ÷ 8

  • Half: 60
  • Half: 30
  • Half: 15 → Answer: 15.

8.4 Paravartya Yojayet (Advanced)

The "Transpose and Apply" method is similar to Nikhilam but works when the divisor is above the base (like dividing by 11, 12, 103, 112).

The Method:
  1. Base is 10. Divisor is 11. Deviation is +1.
  2. Transpose the deviation: +1 becomes -1.
  3. Use the Nikhilam method but with the transposed deviation!

Example: 144 ÷ 12 (Base 10, Deviation +2 → Transpose -2)

  • Split: 14 | 4
  • Bring down 1.
  • 1 × (-2) = -2. Add to next: 4 + (-2) = 2.
  • 2 × (-2) = -4. Add to remainder: 4 + (-4) = 0.
  • Answer: 12 R 0.
