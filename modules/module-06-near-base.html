Imagine calculating 97 × 94 in your head in 3 seconds. Impossible? Not with the "Nikhilam" method! When numbers are close to a "Base" (like 10, 100, 1000), magic happens. This is one of the most famous and powerful Vedic techniques.

6.1 Near 100 (Below Base)

Let's multiply 97 × 94. Both are close to 100. The "Base" is 100.

The Method:
  1. Find the "Deficiency" (how far below 100 each number is).
  2. Left Part of Answer: Cross-subtract (97 - 6 OR 94 - 3). Both give same result!
  3. Right Part of Answer: Multiply the deficiencies (-3 × -6).
Why This Works:

Algebraically, (100-a)(100-b) = 10000 - 100a - 100b + ab = 100(100 - a - b) + ab.
In our example: 100(100 - 3 - 6) + (3×6) = 100(91) + 18 = 9118.
The method perfectly matches the algebra!

Worked Examples:

  1. 89 × 91:
    Deficiencies: -11 and -09
    Left: 89 - 9 = 80 (or 91 - 11 = 80)
    Right: 11 × 9 = 99
    Answer: 8099
  2. 98 × 96:
    Deficiencies: -02 and -04
    Left: 98 - 4 = 94
    Right: 2 × 4 = 08 (Must be 2 digits because Base 100 has 2 zeros!)
    Answer: 9408
Critical Rule: The Right Part must have the same number of digits as zeros in the Base. Base 100 has 2 zeros, so Right Part needs 2 digits (e.g., 8 becomes 08).

6.2 Near 100 (Above Base)

What if numbers are bigger than 100? Like 103 × 108? Same method, but we ADD the "Surplus" instead of subtracting deficiency.

The Method:
  1. Find the Surplus (+3 and +8).
  2. Left Part: Cross-ADD (103 + 8 OR 108 + 3) = 111.
  3. Right Part: Multiply surpluses (3 × 8) = 24.
